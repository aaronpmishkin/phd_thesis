We develop convex reformulations for deep ReLU networks and deep gated ReLU
networks.
Unlike previous work, our approach does not require restricted architectures
or strong assumptions on the input data.
Instead, our proofs rely on a novel layer elimination argument, which shows how
to convert deep (gated) ReLU networks into tensor programs with non-convex
decomposition constraints.
Careful analysis of these constraint sets reveals that they become convex
when the original network layers are sufficiently wide.
Building on our work in \cref{chapter:fast-optimization}, we also extend the
equivalence of ReLU networks and gated ReLU networks with non-singular
activation patterns to arbitrarily many layers.
This leads to an unconstrained convex tensor program which is equivalent
to the non-convex training problem for deep ReLU networks.
