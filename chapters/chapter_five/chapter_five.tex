\chapter{Conclusion}\label{chapter:conclusion}

We leave many problems for future work.
In particular, we do not establish the exact complexity of solving the deep
convex reformulation;
this relies on computing the number of activation patterns achievable in the
\( l^{\text{th}} \) layer of a ReLU network, which have not yet done.
Of particular interest is whether or not low-rank structure in the data
leads to a polynomial number of activation patterns as in the two-layer
setting.
Such a result would imply polynomial time optimization for any deep ReLU
network exceeding the width thresholds \( m^*_l \).

Another open question concerns whether or not our bounds on the width
thresholds are tight.
Although they match the two-layer setting when specialized to \( L = 2 \),
it remains to prove lower bounds for \( L \geq 3 \).
Developing these precise characterizations for \( m_l^* \) is necessary if the
machine learning community is to fully understand the complexity regimes for
training neural networks.

Finally, extending our results to include regularized problems is a critical
