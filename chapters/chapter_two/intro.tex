The goal of this chapter is to take a step towards making optimization for
two-layer neural networks as easy as fitting generalized linear models.
Good optimizers for neural networks should be robust and consistent, come with
optimality and convergence rate guarantees, and be easy to use without
specialist knowledge.
While recent research in optimization has created a fantastic proliferation of
SGMs for training neural networks, new methods generally fail to satisfy even
these basic criteria.
The large number of new methods also creates additional problems for
practitioners, who now must select from a crowded field of competing
algorithms.
This is usually achieved using expensive and time-consuming cross-validation
experiments.

In what follows, we take a markedly different approach and show how to
efficiently and reliably perform global optimization for shallow ReLU networks
by minimizing convex reformulations of the training objective.
Specifically, we develop fast optimization algorithms for two-layer ReLU models
by carefully studying the space of equivalent models.
We prove that unregularized ReLU networks can be trained by decomposing the
solution to an unconstrained generalized linear model (GLM) onto a difference
of polyhedral cones.
With non-zero regularization, the same unconstrained problem yields a
data-dependent approximation of the optimal solution which differs only in the
norm of the model weights.
To fit this GLM, we develop a proximal-gradient method that combines the convex
optimization toolbox with GPU acceleration.
We also use this optimizer as a sub-routine for an augmented Lagrangian method
that quickly and robustly trains ReLU networks via the (constrained) convex
reformulation.
Our deterministic optimizers are parameter-free and give both convergence and
global optimality guarantees.

To summarize, our main contributions are the following:
\begin{itemize}
    \item A new class of \emph{unconstrained} convex optimization problems
          which are equivalent to training an unregularized ReLU model and
          approximation guarantees for the case of non-zero regularization.

    \item An accelerated proximal-gradient method for this unconstrained
          problem that improves the complexity of computing a global optimum from
          \(O(\epsilon^{-2})\) to \(O(\epsilon^{-1/2})\) iterations compared to
          subgradient methods.

    \item An augmented Lagrangian method for the constrained convex
          reformulation which uses our unconstrained solver as a sub-routine and
          outperforms commercial interior-point software such as
          MOSEK~\citep{mosek}.

    \item Extensive experiments which validate our theoretical results,
          carefully explore the properties of our optimization methods, and scale
          convex optimization for ReLU networks to MNIST and CIFAR-10.
\end{itemize}

Quality software is key to practical use of our methods.
As such, we also provide \texttt{scnn}, an open-source package for training
neural networks by convex optimization.%
